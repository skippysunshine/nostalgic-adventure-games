Well, welcome back to Tomahna, my friend! You get to see a fair bit more than you did in Myst III Exile and it’s worth the wait. Some fun puzzles in the Myst style you've come to love (or smash your keyboard over).
     
 

So lets get started – meet Yeesha. She’s played quite well by this young actor with exactly the right amount of pretentiousness I would expect in Atrus and Catherine’s daughter. And when she asks you to take a picture, you can either do as she says – or wait until she gets bored and continues the journey.
     
 

Once the cutscene is finished you can follow Yeesha into Atrus’ workshop by pulling the same lever she did.
Move forward and then watch the cutscene. I note that Atrus’ doesn’t even offer you a drink before putting you to work (and jeez, a decade on Rand Miller is looking better than he did in the original Myst!).
     
 

Atrus asks you to help him calibrate the tuner so zoom in on the panel at the end of the desk (near the window). He will talk you through this first puzzle so it’s not too hard but start by zooming in on the screens at the left hand end of the work bench. The slider will select a waveform and the three dials are, from left to right, amplitude, frequency and phase. Turn the dials slowly and Atrus will tell you when the dial seems set. If he tells you to choose another signal, then move the slider across and start again.

 

If you want to give up, then zooming out twice will start the next event whether you correctly calibrated the tuner or not.
 

In true Myst fashion, working machinery doesn’t function for too long and BANG!! – there goes the power supply. Whilst Atrus heads off to Rime to get some parts, he leaves you the jobs of 1) getting the power back on 2) aligning the roof antenna and 3) caring for his child. In that order!!! No wonder the first two kids turned out a little bitter and twisted.

     
 

There are a lot of journals in Myst IV and they are an interesting read plus give a fair few clues for puzzles so make sure you check the desk and have a read before moving on. Don't forget you can take photos of any interesting pages if you think you might need to view them later (you don't get to take the book with you).
You can have a look around the rest of the workshop but, of course, you'll need to go wandering further afield to solve the Power Puzzle.
 








 
Restoring the Power
 

If you stand at the top of the stairs in the workshop facing the elevator, you'll see a lever to your right. Pull on the lever and jump in. From inside, move the handle with the red lights down one notch and after the elevator has stopped get out for your first close up look at Tomahna proper.

     
 

Follow the walkway round to the right and into the first room. There is a bucket just inside the door where you can test out the effect of ‘clicking and dragging’ the cursor hand on water. A ladder leads down to an small area where a round telescope-looking thing swivels, but does nothing else – wonder if it needs power too?
     
 

Back up the ladder and continue on to the next room (the botany lab). Have a look in there if you want or move ahead and Yeesha will call for you to come and look at a gecko. She tells you about the necklace she is wearing. She also mentions her brothers, which is the first indication that she has had contact with them.
 

Keep on searching (and in Myst fashion, it’s pretty linear). The left fork takes you past a large bedroom (which you can have a look around)to a dead-end round platform and the right path leads to the hydroelectric power generator. Go to the generator and pull the lever to reset the device. Now head back to the botany lab.
  Control Box Puzzle

Once you have been to the hydroelectric power generator and pulled the lever so that the symbols no longer glow, head back to the botany lab. Yeesha tells you that the control box directs power to the different devices in Tomahna. She notes that it’s not lit up correctly at the moment as only a green light on top of a column of lights means a device is getting the right amount of power. (By the way I always let her bugs go free in this lab and she gets cranky).
     
 

Go to the control box and a few clicks will get you the following view. The lights are showing that there is only enough power at the moment to run two devices. How can you tell what they are?? The symbols at the bottom of each column correspond with symbols on devices you've seen in Tomahna. We need to get more power so lets try diverting what power we have to the hydroelectric generator.
     
 

The hydroelectric generator had two symbols and in the control box they are under columns 3 and 4. How do you transfer the power from columns 1 and 5 to columns 3 and 4?? By using the typewriter keys.

The back row of keys tells the control box which column to transfer power from and the front row of keys denotes which column power is sent to. The button on the right activates the power transfer and if you get it all in a mess, just click on the left hand lever to reset.

A bit of playing around will show you how this puzzle works – if you transfer power ‘units’ from a column to a shorter column, then any excess is left behind. You can use this to end up with the correct amounts in columns 3 and 4.
 

For the solution laid out – click here.

     
 

If you have entered correctly, both Columns 3 and 4 should be lit with a green light on top.

Now go back to the hydroelectric generator – the two symbols there will now be lit. Press the button on top and enough water to power a country is released.

Nothing more to do here but wander back for a bit more investigating.
     
 

Oh by the way, if you don't complete these first puzzles in a total of 10 mins, the walkway blows up on your way back and you die….

Naaaaaahhh!! I'm just kidding!!! The walkway collapses no matter what you do, and as usual, its pretty damned hard to die.

 








     
  Tomahna after Dark

Well, that was exciting! And doesn't Tomahna look beautiful by night - well, once you climb up from the wreckage and clear your head.

You are now cut off from Catherine and Atrus' bedroom, so head back to the botany lab. If you look in the Control Box, you will see that everything is now powered. Let Yeesha's bugs out of their glass tank (if you haven't already!) and watch them fly about overhead.

Leave the botany lab and follow the walkway to the next room, where you can climb down a ladder located next to the tub of water (if you climbed down the ladder during the daytime, you may have seen Yeesha playing the flute here). There is a lantern here which is now powered and can be tilted to shine on the family tree engraved in stone. Take a photo!

Back upstairs, head along the walkway and take the elevator up to Atrus' workshop.
 








  Turning on the roof antenna
 

We should probably get that antenna turned on, like Atrus asked, and let him know that we've blown up half his property and aren't really too sure where his only daughter is!

When you were here last, did you take a photo of the coloured crystal picture in Atrus' journal?? If not, search the desk drawers and get that photo.
 

Now to turn on the antenna! The first step is to pull the lever that opens a panel in the roof. Having trouble finding it?? That's because the Myst games often have a recommended brightness and contrast that render some items almost invisible. If you take the stairs up to the mid level walkway in the workshop and turn back towards the elevator you should see the lever (it doesn't work until you have the power fully restored i.e. night time).
 

Walk up the few steps to the highest level in the workshop and sit in the chair at the end. Press the red flashing button (note it's easy to accidentally leave the chair trying to press this button). You'll be lifted up closer to the telescope and will need to zoom in.

Press the top button on the right - it has a picture of a tall light globe on it and will start to glow orange. This is all you need to do but if you want, you can use the wheels to the left and right of the large viewer to move the telescope until it is filled with the moon.

Press the red chair button again to leave and walk back down to the machine that Atrus was working on when the power blew. The machine is thrumming (is that a word?) and showing static on it's viewer - zoom in!
 

Underneath the viewer are 5 small shuttered compartments with an orange button in front of each. On the inside of the door to the right you will be able to see a slider and a lever that completes a half circle over coloured dots.

The idea here is to replicate the 5 crystals pictured in Atrus' journal in the nearby desk.

The slider reveals a selection of crystal shapes and the lever allows you to select the colour.

Here's the full solution.
     
 
 

So, Atrus left instructions to go to his bedroom and press the button on his desk to view his journals on the Haven and Spire ages plus he asked us to remind Yeesha to do her homework.......... Well, let's do the former and see if the kid turns up in the meantime!
 

Since the walkway to Atrus and Catherine's bedroom is in serious need of repair at the moment we need to find another way to get there. Fortunately, we now have full power on Tomahna so lets jump in the elevator here and see where we can get to.

Enter the elevator (lever to open the elevator door is at the top of the short stairs leading to the lift). Once in the lift slide the lever all the way down, lighting up the lowest red button.

You'll be taken on a short ride and end up on the platform next to Atrus' bedroom. Exit the elevator and go straight up the stairs and into his bedroom - go a step further if you want to view the wreckage of the walkway.
 

Once in the bedroom, you can view a note on a bedside table where the names are written in D'ni - take a photo for later.

Two lamps shine brightly and the fireplace seems to have a bright blue object in it. Enter the fireplace and you will retrieve Yeesha's necklace.
 

The necklace becomes available in your inventory at the bottom of the screen and clicking on it shows that it has recorded a 'memory' of the moment when Yeesha lost it.

What's that girl up to?? She's supposed to be doing homework!

  
 

Head back out of the fireplace and over to the desk. Open the cupboard at the top centre of the desk and zoom in to view a drawing showing that the fireplace contains a code panel which can be reset by turning the lamps off and back on. Zoom back out and click on the square underneath the cupboard.

This reveals two hidden journals (note the button only works when the cupboard above is open and that your cursor hand will not change to the button click position).
 

Have a look through the journals and you will find there are crystal combinations that you can use to look at those ages through the viewer in Atrus' workshop. However, it's not necessary to do that - I only just found out those books are there!

    
 








  Fireplace Puzzle

Enter the fireplace in Atrus and Catherine's bedroom (and retrieve the memory necklace if you haven't already). You must have restored power to Tomahna and have both lamps turned on in the bedroom to attempt this puzzle.

Once in the fireplace, you will see lasers from two directions shining red lights onto the wall. Press the button and a panel will lower, showing the lights hitting a grid.

The aim is to ensure all the squares touched by a laser light are red. When you click on a square, the adjoining north, south, east and west squares will turn red.

Full solution here.
 

Once you have gone down in the fireplace elevator, you will be presented with a golden sphere. Look at the heavy door and listen to the memory the necklace raises then pull the lever in front of the sphere. Enter the sphere and push the small gold lever.

Two linking books are presented and you have a choice of which age you wish to visit first:
 
Spire

or

Haven
 








  The Serenia Linking Book
 

Once you have completed either the Spire age or the Haven age and you link back to Tomahna, you will view a little cutscene from inside the golden sphere.

A man appears on a balcony with a linking book, disappears into that age and the linking book falls to the decking below.

If you want to check out that linking book and go to Serenia, you need to complete the Bookcase puzzle. But I would recommend leaving it until you have finished both Haven and Spire.

    
 








  The Kitchen

If you haven't already head over to the kitchen. To get there, take the elevator to the platform near Atrus' bedroom (it's the lowest red light setting inside the elevator) and then walk around behind the elevator capsule.

At the gap in the railing, push the lever to the right and the walkway will swivel around, giving you access to the kitchen. Have a good look around and a play with what moves (you can make some okay music on the pots in the kitchen and play with the taps and stoves. Use the bellows to pump up the fire in the fireplace). Take a photo of the alphabet book on the table for later.
 








  Yeesha's Bedroom
 

Back to the platform, you can again move a lever to swivel the walkway - the next destination is Yeesha's bedroom below. Again, have a wander around and look at things, noting the memories the necklace shows as you go. The fish tank is pretty cool, as is the wooden pinball game. Yeesha's journal is on the bed. You may need to move a pillow to find it (unless you have seen the cutscene from the golden linking sphere).
     
 








  Bookcase Puzzle
 

Yeesha's journal on her bed reveals the crystal code for you to view the final age of Serenia on the viewer in Atrus' workshop. Again, it's not compulsory to do this. Yeesha also notes that she and Atrus changed the lock on her bookcase and that she now knows how to spell the family members names in D'ni. Yeesha is happy that she will no longer forget who is older than who in her family tree.
 

That's the biggest clue on what you need to do next. If you have played around with the bookcase, you may have realised that the names of the family members are written in the D'ni language on some of the books' spines and that you can push in five books before the system resets.

The aim here is to select the 5 books that represent the family members in their order of age from oldest to youngest.

The full solution is here
 


Once you have clicked on the correct books, the bookcase will slide down and a ladder to the lower deck will be revealed. Head on down and have a look around this small area.
 

Now, if you have completed either the Spire Age or the Haven Age, you will have been shown a cutscene of a man disappearing from the balcony outside Yeesha's room and dropping a linking book down here. You can now go to the linking book and go to the final age - Serenia.

If you haven't completed one of the other worlds, there isn't alot down here for you to do yet. Head back to Haven or Spire.
