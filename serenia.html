Welcome to Serenia! I'm so excited because in the two or three times I played the game, I never tried linking back out of Serenia via the book next to the pedestal where you first appear. Hence, three years later while writing this walk through, I have discovered that you can link back from here to the spot you saw Catherine and baby Yeesha during Myst Exile III!! I'm pathetically excited about that, and a bit miffed I never noticed it before! I did wonder why they hadn't replicated that part of Tomahna.
       
 


So what can you expect here?? Well, more freakishly hard puzzles but loads of atmosphere and fun in true Myst fashion. If you don't already have it on, go into options and activate the Zip mode function. You'll get around Serenia much more easily if you do.
 


Believe it or not, this world is curvier and harder to describe than Edanna in Myst III! Here's a rough map if you get stuck.
       
 

So I assume you have had a wander around and seen a few of the sights on Serenia. Places of interest include the water devices, the floating bubbles, the floating spores, the mini-tornadoes, those weird water dudes. Perhaps you have also seen the memory chambers, the harvesters and even had a chat with Achenar......

 








 


Lowering the Drawbridge
       
 

First step is to make sure you have access to all the main areas, so that means working out how to lower the drawbridge blocking the path through that tall rock. It's easy to do but hard to work out what actually makes it happen.

You need to go to the open stone platform where you can see across to some other islands and then up the stairs behind you. You will see Achenar climb up out of a gully onto a broken bridge. He'll talk to you (giving you a clue where to find his old journal). Then he'll run out of sight and the drawbridge will be open when you walk (or better yet zip) all the way back around.

 








 


The Root Chamber
       
 

Achenar climbed out of the gully with a life stone in his hands. You can get into the Root Chamber to have a look around (but there is actually nothing to do there that affects the game at all). After Achenar has told you about his journal and run off, turn to your left and note the totem pillar with the long red lever. Pull the lever and you will hear what sounds like stone sliding across stone.

Move back down the stone steps behind you and you will see an open manhole at their base.

 

Head down the manhole and continue down to floor level. You can see the pedestals with life stones on them (and of course, one is missing). There is a reflector that you can play with to direct sunlight around the chamber but it has no other effect within the game.

You can also exit the chamber the way Achenar did, by going through the narrow crevasse. That places you on the drawbridge side of the gully, where you can access the old memory chamber and working harvester.

Once you commence solving other puzzles, the Root Chamber becomes flooded with water, so it wont always be a shortcut across the gully - that's where the Zip option comes in handy.
 








 


Finding Achenar's Journal
 

Achenar said his journal was in a pillar at the place where two rivers cross. Use the Zip option to go to the intersection of the two rivers. Turn around 180° and move forward one step down the stone path and turn to your left. The large stone pillar is dimly lit but the hand will turn into a magnifying glass when you pass over it. Zoom in and open the box to reveal Achenar's journal.
   
       
 


The Working Harvester
 

So head down through the now lowered drawbridge and take the steps down to a darkened door beneath the old memory chamber (which is, of course, locked). Follow the path to see a small wooden platform, a waterwheel in the distance and a couple of levers.

The large lever on the left raises and lowers a harvester, similar to the one Sirrus destroyed. The lever on the right opens the hatch door. Move both levers now and then step along the platform and you will be able to hop inside the harvester and have a look around.

Not too much to see but one of those annoyances of the Myst series exists here. If you set your game to the recommended brightness level, you probably have no chance of seeing the lever that closes the hatch from inside the harvester! It's at the top of the open doorway.

Pull the hatch door shut and turn around 180° and pull the lever above the porthole to lower the harvester.
       
 
 

As you descend down in the pool, it's interesting to note that there seems to be a door halfway down and what could be memory globes at the bottom, but there's not too much you can do from here. Pull the lever over the porthole to go back up.

Looks like we'll have to drain the water in this pool to get access to that door.
 

Head back to Water Device 1 and move the wheel to the right. The water should stop flowing on your left.

Now go to Water Device 2 and climb up the ladder. Use the closest lever to roll the whole device to the right, onto the shaft. Then step behind it and roll the lever all the way to the right. Step back towards the ladder and wheel the whole device back to the left off the shaft.

Go down the ladder and move one step along the wooden path. When you look at the machine, you'll be able to zoom in (for some reason!) on the cogs, but to the right of that is a toggle on a rope that you need to pull down until the flow of water to your left is stopped - I think you need to pull it twice to completely block off the flow.
       
 

Now head over to the open stone platform where you saw Achenar climb up out of the gully with the life stone. At the top of the stairs, follow the wooden path to Water Device 4.

Here's another of those winged snakes that you saw on Haven (and as a matter of interest, this is the only piece of info from Haven that you use further on in the game). Stroke the lower stripe of the snake four times and click on it's head when it looks at you. Once it is gone, turn the wheel all the way to left.
       
 

Now technically, you have adjusted all the devices that you need to, to allow you to progress with the working harvester. However, I discovered while writing this walk through, that the game is coded to ensure you can't actually do any more in the harvester pool, even after correctly setting Water Devices 1, 2 and 4, until you have spoken with the protector, Caradell. She appears at the edge of the stone platform after you have both spoken to Yannin and removed the winged snake lock from Water Device 4. Have a listen to what Caradell says but there is no rush to get to the hall of spirits yet, so head back to the working harvester.
       
 

Back at the harvester, raise it to the surface. Open the hatch and you will see a panel move in the rock near the entrance to the harvester. Walk over there and even though it is hard to make out, you will be able to step down to the right of the wooden platform.

You end up in a stone stairway which heads down to a closed wooden door.

One step back from the wooden door, there is a porthole into the harvester pool and a wheel. Turn the wheel and a door opposite will open, draining more water from the pool. But you need to get the water level even lower to get past the wooden door in this stairway.

Head back up and lower the harvester once more. Whilst it is lowered, open the hatch - you'll hear the bubbles of air escaping. Close the hatch and then raise the harvester (quite realistically, I had to struggle back and forth with the large lever to raise the harvester - that was probably because I tried it too quickly but the effect was still good). Once the harvester is up, you should be free to travel down the stone stairway to a locked metal door. A memory from the necklace lets you know that one of Sirrus' colour puzzles is involved here. Click on the button and a dial of coloured dots is revealed.

You have a colour pattern from the medallion in Spire but you'll need more info from Serenia to solve this puzzle.
   
 


Hall of Spirits
 

Conveniently, the Hall of Spirits is one of the places saved in your Zip mode, so Zip there and head up the stairs. To get any action underway here, you must have spoken to the protector on the open stone platform. She is triggered to appear after you have removed the winged snake from Water Device 4 (and possibly been down in the Root Chamber).

In the hall you will see three masked sisters seated and 'in the dream'. A sister will explain that they are trying to locate Yeesha but the message they are getting in the dream makes no sense. In fact, they suspect the message is for you!
       
 

However, it would be impossible for you to travel in the dream without a guide, and they need to assess which type of guide you are (take note, makers of Syberia I and II - this is how you incorporate puzzles into a story!). Click on the cloth and you will be declared to be either:
 
 

A child of Wind

A child of Water

A child of Fire
       
 

The sister will explain to you that you need to make an offering to get the guide to accompany you in the 'dream'. The following are the correct offerings for each of the guides above:
       
 
 

Pollen Spores

Bubbles

Burning Sap
       
 

Okay, so the idea is that you need to collect the relevant offering and deliver it to your guide. This is made a little more difficult, in that the guides do tend to move about a bit. However, you can be guaranteed that all will appear somewhere in the stone forest and that they will appear in similar surroundings e.g. the water guide always appears in a round pool near a Water Device, the wind guide will always appear in a barren grassy area with small stones etc.

The relevant offering for a guide will never be more than four steps away. The tricky bit is if you move your hand too quickly, the offering will disappear. Also, if you dont find the correct guide in four steps, the offering will disappear on the fifth step you take. Might take you a few goes and you might need to move around the stone forest a bit, but it's not too hard overall. You just need to locate the possible locations where your particular guide may appear and the sources of correct offering near those locations.

I was deemed to be a child of the wind (ahem!) in my most recent game. I found three locations where the wind tornadoes appeared within the stone forest. I knew there were spore trees within four steps of two locations, so I would scare off the guide from the third location, then go and get a spore and approach one of the other locations. It took me five attempts but I got there in the end. Apparently, being deemed a child of fire is the rarest.
 

Once you have made the correct offering, you will be rewarded with a shower of sparks. This shows my offering to the Wind Guide being accepted.

  
       
       
 


Dreaming in The Memory Chamber
 

Once you have presented an offering to your correct guide, head back to the new memory chamber. When you travel up the stairs inside the memory chamber, the sister will be waiting to invite you to lay down and dream. A nice interlude accompanied by Peter Gabriel ensues and I hope you enjoy it because you can't cut it short!

People talk about this puzzle being easy and fun but it bugs the shit out of me everytime I try it and I invariably revert to the save game from the one time I actually did it!

You will eventually end up back in control viewing a very bright white light that represents Yeesha. Your job is to convert the coloured ancestors' lights around her white as well.

You will find as you pass your hand over a light it will change colour (except for Yeesha's). Here are some tips to help you along:
 

The lights change colour in the following sequence:
Red, purple, blue, cyan, green, yellow, white.
       
 

If you backtrack to a light you just changed, it wont have any immediate effect. But if you stay on it too long or pass across it to a new light, it makes some of the lights around it change.
       
 

If you make too many errors, you will hear a noise and a few of your already white lights will revert back to colours.
       
 

I found the best approach was to circle around between 3 or 4 lights and slowly make my way around. So from Yeesha's central white light, I passed the hand one to the right, then one down, then one to the left and then back up to Yeesha's light. I repeated that until another light turned white and then excluded that from my rotation and brought another one in. Sometimes I just rotated between 3 lights in a triangle pattern.

You will often have to turn a white light back to red to keep your rotation going but eventually you will move around and make all the lights white (and if not, you will search on the web and find someone else's save game where they have completed that puzzle).
       
       
       
       
       
  The Coloured Dot Door
 

Ahhhhh! Coloured dots on a door looks a bit familiar! This door is revealed once you have drained the water from the functioning harvester's pool. If you are yet to complete the dreaming in the new memory chamber, you wont get very far here. In fact, if you click on the necklace when zoomed in on this door, you will just see the door and hear a voice over of Achenar and Sirrus discussing that Sirrus should design one of his colour code puzzles to lock the door.

If you have completed the dreaming (including the light puzzle), then clicking on the necklace here will give you the vital clue you need to unlock the door.

 

The necklace reminds you of the medallion in Spire (which you should have taken a photo of to record the colour pattern - it'll still be back on the elevator at the top of Spire if you forgot or if you used someone else's save game to get through the dreaming!). The necklace then reveals two groups of 3 colours and shows that when those 3 colours are placed together, they produce white light.
 

Okay, so the idea is that you move the dots around the available slots by clicking on a dot and selecting a place to move it to from the paths that will be outlined in white for you. There is an inner circle of 6 dots (they form the 'tops' of little triangles). These 6 dots need to replicate the pattern that was on the medallion in Spire from the game you are playing! It changes for every game so all I can give you is an example below:

I have then completed the triangles according to the colour associations the necklace revealed i.e. Red, green, and blue dots go together and purpley-pink, cyan, and yellow dots go together.
       
       
       
       
       
  In the Old Memory Chamber
 

Once you have the coloured door open, head through the tunnel and up the ladder. Have a look around this area - you'll find a wooden door that you can rattle but not unlock (is this the one Sirrus came out of before to warn you about Achenar??). There is also a flower in a chamber, although it looks nowhere near as healthy as the one in the new memory chamber - you can pull the red handle and go inside the flower chamber for a look but there doesn't seem to be much to do. Also in this area is a metal sarcophagus - if you zoom in on the viewing glass you will see a frozen Sirrus - the only time he's not overacting in the whole game!
 
SAVE YOUR GAME HERE!
 
       
       
 

Onwards, up the wooden stairs to be swept into a cutscene. You've finally found Yeesha! And she encourages you to free her before Achenar returns. After an agonising wait for your hand cursor to return, interspersed with nagging from Yeesha, click to move forward and look at the control panel.

 

Yet another cutscene takes over and now you have a dilemma - do you believe Achenar or do you believe Yeesha??

 

Three possible outcomes here:
 

Do nothing: Yeesha is revealed to actually be Sirrus - she kills Achenar with his own bow and then kills you!

Pull the Silver Lever: Yeesha is revealed to actually be Sirrus and she kills you both with a large memory stone

Pull the Amber Lever: Right move! Yeesha's unconcious so we wont be hearing from her or Sirrus again! Woohoo! Game over!

  Rescuing Yeesha from the Dreaming
 

Of course, I guess you don't need to finish there if you dont want. After Achenar's talk, sit in the chair and look up above you to the dreaming stone.
 

Your spirit guide is still hanging around and it explains that there is an interloping evil ladybeetle that prevents Yeesha from leaving the dreaming.

The next puzzle shows five items with some icons floating over them. When you pass your cursor over an icon, you hear a piece of a conversation.

The aim here is to sort the conversation into the correct order, whilst ensuring each piece of conversation is floating over an item relevant to it.

When you click on one or more icons over an item, you pick up the whole group. Then as you pass over another item, the top icon sticks to that item. You can't immediately put an icon back on the item you just picked it up from - you need to drop at least one icon on another item first.
 

Here are the five items and the conversation pieces in no particular order:

Yeesha: You can make one just like you made my spirit guide.

Yeesha: I'll tell them to when I link home.

Sirrus: I'd like to sis, but I don't have a chess set.

Yeesha: Daddy's really good at chess.

Sirrus: That way, I'll never be able to break it.

Yeesha: You should play him.

Sirrus: They break so easily.

Yeesha: Well, maybe mum and dad could give you a set, as a present.

Sirrus: I could, but it's really hard to carve figurines that small.

Sirrus: Ha, right, and I suppose you'll tell them to make it out of the same rock as this chamber.
