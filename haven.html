Here's a link to a map of Haven with some of the key locations highlighted.

The first thing you will note is that the linking book back to Tomahna used to be contained in the caged off area of a small room where visitors could pass items through to people in that world. However, something serious has gone down here and the room has been destroyed. If you have already been to Spire, this will look familiar. The necklace has a couple of stories for you here, one of which shows Yeesha in some difficulty. Pull the handle to hear that cool horn.
       
 

Step outside and there is only one direction to move in – play with the crab if you like – it wont stick around for long. Take the path up into the rock. You’ll end up looking at some interesting rock art. Swivel around to look at your options and firstly take the path that slopes up steeply with rope hand-holds. Great view of the jungle here and you can make out some future points of interest such as a building and what looks like a ship’s mast. That’s all there is to do here so back down to the rock art and then down the ladder to the beach.
Only one way to go here – on the right you’ll see a skeleton and the necklace will provide sound effects. Zoom in on the totem pole and take a photo of the diamond symbol.

 
 








 


Getting onto the ship

Keep going toward the ship until you are inside the small wooden elevator (with the wheel and levers). The aim here is create a counter weight that is heavy enough to lift you (and anything you need to take up with you).

       
 

Exit the elevator and go down the stairs to the right. You’ll see some cannonballs down there – just use your hand to roll them into the counterweight box. By running back and winding the wheel and pulling the big lever in the elevator, you will work out how many are needed to operate the lift.

Once you have gone up in the elevator, you won’t be able to get much further. Turns out you need to take up something to weigh down that panel. Slide the small lever to go back down in the elevator and start experimenting with the box and cannonballs closest to the elevator.

If you slide some balls into the closest box and then slide that whole box onto the elevator, you’ll need to adjust the counterweight to compensate for the extra weight. A little experimenting should give you the right answer. If not, the correct answer is here.

       
 

Head across to the ship and you'll see some stock pirate treasure plus a ladder to climb up and a rope net to travel down. Climb up and get a close look at a karnak. Click on it to shoo it away and walk forward. You can turn the handle on your right and a sail will unfurl. Zoom in and take a photo.

When done, slide the small lever and investigate the small opening on the other side. Look in Achenar's journal and you’ll be able to learn the names of five creatures that live in Haven: Karnak, Mangree, Camoudile, Zeftyr and a Cerpatee.

When done, climb down the ladder and then move across and climb down again to the lowest level of the ship. Take a photo of the footprints on the desk and your work here is done. Back to the elevator and slide the small level to go back down.

     
 

Head back along the beach and climb back up the ladder to the rock art. Now go along the most level path and look at more rock art. Touch the picture nailed to the crates to view some house designs. Click on the tent and a panicked Zeftyr will send you on a little journey into the forest.

     
 








 


In the Forest

As your vision slowly clears you’ll see a very curious interloper poking you. It hears a warning cry and takes off, quickly followed by a friend and a pursuing camoudile. If you look down, you will be able to see the camoudile footprint and take a photo if you want.

There are a few directions to go here (map available here). Lets go up past the tent first (and if you zoom in on the tent remains caught up in the tree, the necklace will show you the trip out into the forest again).

Move two steps from the tent and you will reach a 'T' intersection. Going right takes you back to the cliff where you started your tent ride, so go left and you will end up at a locked gate.
 








 


Unlocking the Gate

Zoom in on the panel next to the gate and lift up the cover to reveal the puzzle that unlocks the gate (you can also listen with the necklace here). You’ve probably already spent lots of time fiddling around so my first hint to you is that the gate will unlock if one of the little hammers is resting on each of the white pebbles when the pebble is sitting outside it’s row’s slider.
The second hint is that the sliders will only slide when a hammer is resting in one of its slots and the pebbles don’t move at all. Here’s the full solution.

Once you are through the gate, you’ll find a nice view of the lagoon and another totem pole to zoom in on and photograph.
 








 


Viewing the Mangree Totem
 

From where you landed in the forest and could view the tent hanging in the tree, turn 90 degrees to the right and head 5 steps along the path.

It looks as though you are at a dead end with bushes on all sides but swivel around and look up and you will see a viewing tower. Hold your hand over the orange reeds in front of you and you will be able to move two aside, allowing you to move in and climb up to the platform.

 

Once on the platform, you will see three wheels. Have a play and listen to the differing tones. There is another totem pole in the distance but when you zoom in, those pesky mangrees are doing a very good job of covering the symbol.

The aim here is to replicate the warning call the mangrees use to scare them off the totem pole.

 

The first hint is to think of the tones as either long or short. A long tone requires one full turn of the correct wheel and a short tone takes about a 1/3 turn. Turn the wheels at a moderate (relaxed) speed.

The second hint is that the mangree warning does not need the middle wheel.

The full solution is here.
 

Once you have scared off the mangrees and zoomed in for a photo of the totem, turn around and look at the entrance to the viewing platform. Place your hand above the opening and you will be able to pull down and photograph a picture of the Haven food chain represented by footprints. By the sounds of the necklace, it should have a huge Achenar footprint at the top!
     
 








 


Finding the Camoudile Totem

From where you land and can view the tent hanging in the tree, turn around 180 degrees and head 2 steps down the path. You will view a couple of Zeftyrs here.

As you move forward the zeftyrs will make a run for it but only one of them gets away. Whilst the camoudile is munching away on his dinner, go past and to your left at the end of the path are some weird looking plants. The camoudile will politely show you the effect these plants can have.

If you head back the way you came, there is a path in the tall grass to the right. Go around the boulder and zoom in on the totem to photograph the symbol.
 








 



The Swamp and the Zeftyr Totem

To find the swamp and the zeftyr totem, stand facing the locked (or now maybe unlocked!) gate. Turn 90 degrees to your left and follow the path up into the swamp area.

I thought this area was quite beautiful with many creatures to look at and it seems to be a favourite haunt of the zeftyrs. Step along the wooden planks and you will keep your feet dry! When in the middle of 3 planks take the plank on your left side and you will find another totem with a symbol to photograph.

Go back and keep moving deeper into the swamp until you get to the lagoon. Head up the stairs and have a look around. It looks as though a section of the walkway has been raised – guess who has to work out how to lower it.
 








 


The Lagoon Totem Puzzle
 

Okay, so how do you get the walkway lowered so you can continue on your journey??

At the end of this pier is a wooden box with a lever that doesn’t do much..... yet. Look up at the ceiling and you will see a handle you can pull down. This lifts the lid from a weird looking machine.

There are 5 knobs on the top of this machine and when you pull each one up, it scrolls through a display of symbols (although one viewer is broken).

Hint 1: Display 5 symbols in the correct order and push the lever on the left to lower the walkway.

Hint 2: The 5 symbols you need to use are the ones you photographed on the five totems around Haven

Hint 3: Each symbol represents an animal – The totem with that symbol would have been located in the area that animal most likes to inhabit.

Hint 4: The order in which you display the symbols is based on the food chain on Haven – the animals from the top to the bottom of the food chain should be represented on this machine from left to right.

Hint 5: To work out all of the above, go here.

 

Walk along the walkway to the hut and go in through the dodgy door. Move to the trunk on your left and open the lid. Zoom in on the inside of the lid and take a photo. Have a look through another of Achenar’s journals, noting that the Mangrees have names for each other and even made one up for him!

On the desk is a rolled up drawing. Use your hand to unroll it and take a photo.

Time to head upstairs. Climb the pole and have a look around. There is another drawing on the table to photograph and also one behind the painting on the easel (you need to hold your cursor over the bottom left of the painting before the hand changes and you can lift the painting to see underneath.

Leave the hut and continue further along the walkway. I got a scare the first time that aggressive camoudile growled at me! There is a ladder down but you will have to get rid of the camoudile first. Keep walking along till you get to the tower and climb up.
 








 


That @#%!#&* Monkey Nest Puzzle!
 

Okay, first thing is to save your game so you can go back and work through the puzzle from the beginning as laid out here. You can also reset the puzzle by going back and entering Achenar's hut on the lagoon.

Have a good look around up in the tower overlooking the mangree nests - in particular note the drawing of a mangree throwing fruit that is behind you. Fiddle with anything that moves and if you manage to solve this puzzle with no assistance whatsoever, then you probably designed it. This is one of those ones that made my blood boil until I realised that life is too short and went to a hint guide. I ended up getting hints from basic to the full solution and still had trouble getting it done (however, in the original un-patched game, the camoudile took much less time to scrabble out of the hole than it does in version 1.03!).

After playing around for a while you may find that the mangrees respond to certain combinations of sounds created by turning the wheels. When their ‘song’ is played, they will run to an empty nest that is directly connected by rope. Also, when the mangrees run around they attract the attention of the camoudile below. The aim is to get the camoudile out of action so you can go down there.

Each of the mangrees is recognisable by a distinguishing feature and responds to its own particular call. The camoudile will follow the moving mangrees and stay under their nest until the next mangree makes a move. One particular move causes the camoudile to fall into a hole and it takes a while to scramble its way back out. Beside the hole are some of those weird looking plants that you saw in the tall grass near the camoudile totem.

Here’s what needs to happen: you need to use the wheels to call mangrees in the correct order to ensure that the camoudile falls in the hole and then, before it has time to scramble out, get the Grandpa mangree into the leftmost nest and have him throw a fruit at the spore plants, causing the camoudile to pass out. Simple huh?

Here is the solution.
     
 

Time to climb down from the tree house and back along the path to where you first saw the camoudile below. Approaching from the treehouse shows you that there is a ladder going down, so go down. Pass the sick camoudile and cross the clearing (I kept wondering if the mangrees would peg fruit at me!).
Climb up the next structure and photograph the drawing on the leaves. Follow the walkway (make sure to look behind you as you go!) and take a seat in the chair.

You need to get rid of the snake but the necklace will give you a memory of how to do it. The trick is to left-click, stroke and then release and do that four times. Don’t stroke too slowly or it won’t work. When the snake looks at you, click once on it’s head and it will go away.

Pull the lever and go for a ride. Haven is completed so go into the destroyed chamber and link back to Tomahna.

     
     
     
 

By the way, you can complete the game without going to Haven at all. But wasn't it worth it??
