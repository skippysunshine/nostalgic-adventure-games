Welcome to Spire - it's eerily beautiful and has a little too much overacting by Sirrus. This is a small world with very difficult puzzles so lets learn where everything is first.
     
 

Looks like there’s been a little action going on here - the linking chamber has seen better days (and if you have been to Haven already, this may look a little familiar). Don't forget to pull on the horn - it doesn't help in the game but it sounds cool! Head down the stairs and you will pass an elevator (not working).

 

Keep going and you will step down into an open area. A path leads off to the right to an elevator that does operate but lets leave it for getting back up, not going down.
The sculpture is worth a closer look. Where did you see that scene before?? In a painting in Atrus and Catherine's bedroom on Tomahna - nice call back to the orginal Myst too. Next to the sculpture is an old sleeping roll and a memory on your necklace.
     
 

Move to your left and at the end of the path you’ll see a rock with a crystal embedded on top. Below the crystal is a metal measuring ruler. Take a photo.
There’s a hole to your right and as you can’t do much else up here, you may as well jump in!


Slide out into an open area then go forward and look left. You’ll see what looks to be a similar structure floating in the distance and some floating crystals in the foreground. Move further along and pull the lever to check out the printed circuit board that sparks and zaps and doesn't do much else at this stage.

     
 

A thick green cable leads to a pedestal with what looks like a bread box on top. Press the blue button on the left side of the pedestal and the lid rolls back to reveal some sort of gadget. Have a play to see if you get anything happening. Over the left hand side is a large electromagnet.


Behind you is a pole with climbing rungs. Climb up and use your hand to open the hatch. On this level are Sirrus’ sleeping quarters on one side of the ladder and his laboratory on the other.

 

Have a look around the laboratory. You can play with some items on the desk: move a floating crystal or create electricity (slide the lever and wind the wheel) or look through the funky crystal telescope. Hmmm, that does look like another island floating off in the distance.
 

Heading back towards the ladder, you’ll note you can take a path to the right to get on an elevator. Call the elevator and go down to the lowest level. You wont go far before you come up against a locked gate. Looks interesting in there.

Back up the elevator to investigate the far side of this level where Sirrus had a bed and a desk. Have a look around and photograph anything that looks informative – lots of notes on paper here. Don’t miss the compartment on the desk which reveals 2 more sheets. There is also another rock with a crystal embedded on top - get as close as you can and zoom in for a look at it. The metal ruler part is missing! Turn around and look back towards the bed and chess board. Pass your hand over one of the bedposts and you'll get a magnifying glass, allowing you to zoom in and view the metal measuring ruler. Must have been one hell of an explosion to embed it there! Take a photo.

Go back to face the desk and then move one step to the right to find an inviting green hole. Jump on in – it’s Myst! You rarely die doing stuff like this!
       
 

When you arrive at the bottom you will realise you are inside the room you couldn’t get to earlier because of the locked gate. Zoom in on the statue and take a photo of the information there. Take a seat and press the blue button. The console that lowers down has lots of confusing symbols and buttons. Depending on what you have done elsewhere some or none of the 36 little crystals may be lit. Nothing else works so time to move on - press the blue button again to raise the chair console. I would recommend however, that when you get a reaction with other consoles on Spire, that you come back and see if anything has changed with this chair.

You can open the gate from the inside so exit the room and take the elevator up one level (you can also take it up another level if you ever want to leave and link back to Tomahna). Once back on the laboratory/sleeping level, walk to the pole ladder and climb up to the top level.
       
 








  The First Spire Puzzle
 

So, now you've had a good explore around Spire, what on earth are you supposed to do first?? The first puzzle involves the small pod that seems to float via the pull of two large electromagnets.

 

At the very top of the pole ladder is a console with four floating crystals connected. There is a magnet up above to match the one way down below and a small oval pod thing floating in the air.
Press the blue button to roll back the lid. Flick the toggle on the left side of the machine. On the right, a level indicator lets you know where the pod is in relation to the two magnets. There is also a small green dot to show what level the pod needs to be at. 
       
 

Flick the toggle on the left to power the machine. Play around to discover that you get to move this slider 3 times before power is drawn from the floating crystals to the magnet: how much power depends on which 3 moves you make with the slider. As the pod needs to float down 5 increments for you to board it, the aim here is to draw power from two floating crystals up here and 7 from the crystals floating downstairs. With a little experimentation you will work out that the slider has 3 positions and you have 3 adjustments before power is transferred from the 4 floating conductors above you.

Here's the full solution.
       
 








  The Other Island
 

Finally! You've got the pod in the right position for boarding (and if you haven't, you need to go here first). It looks much bigger once it gets down close, doesn't it? Climb on in and push the lever forward. Quick trip!
 

When you get to the other island, climb out the top and have a look around. Move forward and you will see there is another console linked to a fat green cable and stacks of floating crystals.... hmmmm doesn't look as though you can jump that gap though. Follow the path and note yet another crystal hole - the necklace shows a couple of Sirrus stories in this section - he ends up shaking his fists about something in an outfit that is way too clean and pressed for someone living on Spire.

Down the hole and head over to look at the telescope suspended in a net of chains.
Use the directional push buttons to scan the view and when the blue button is glowing, push it to zoom in. You should view a few things: another metal measuring ruler (take a photo); what looks like a winch or pulley system and a blurry tangle of thin cables.


 

Head over to the large wheel and pull the lever - nothing happens. Look closely on the right hand side and note that there is a piece of green stone jamming the wheel. Click on it and it will float away, leaving you free to pull the lever and wonder what's being unwound.
 


Head back up the rope and lo and behold! There is now a huge chunk of floating green rock that you can walk on to get across the gap.

Go straight ahead first and open up the printed circuit board. It displays the same amount of lights as the one back on the main island. That means everything must be connected.

Head over to the console and open that sucker up! This one has 34 floating crystals connected!! Gonna be hard to light them all - lets try lighting as many as possible.

Solution is here.

Your work here is done. Head back to the pod and use the lever to travel back to the main island.
       
 








  The Spider Chair Puzzle
 

The following assumes you have done all of the actions described above, including travelling to the other island and conducting power from 29 of the floating crystals there; photographed all of the metal measuring rulers; photographed all of the information sheets on Spire and currently have 38 lights glowing on the printed circuit board.
       
 

So! Rock on down to the spider chair, sit in it and press the blue button. The console lowers and finally, all of the little green crystals light up!!
 

....... and then the power blows!! Maybe you are now providing too much power to the chair? Count the little green lights to discover there are 36. What's the easiest way to reduce the power by two??
 

Press the blue button and exit the room via the gate. Take the elevator up one level and then walk to the pole ladder. Climb up to the top and open the console. Flick the toggle switch on the left and the system will stop drawing 2 units of power.

Head back down to the spider chair (make sure you flip down the circuit breaker that blew, before you sit back down). Press the blue button and you should now have an active chair console.

    
 

You thought that was hard??? Oh my god...... it only gets worse.
 

Slide the blue button on the left of the console down a slot and the chair will descend as will that creepy statue of Atrus and Catherine.
       
 








  Tuning Spire
 

So, you've descended one level in the Spider chair, along with the creepy statue. The next step along the way is to tune those cables you spied via the telescope on the other island.

Slide the blue slider on the chair down another slot and the spider chair will rotate and descend to a weird view of lots of what I guess are crystals but look like sad ghosts.

At the bottom centre of the spider chair console is a panel with light blue symbols on it. Click on the panel and then on the rectangular blue button that is revealed.

You will descend much further down under Spire and you really hope that Sirrus built this stuff well!
 

Have a look at the three mechanisms. Each has a symbol, which relates to the large crystals with measuring rulers above.
 
 
Pentagonal Crystal
Position 4
Circular Crystal
Position 3
Hexagonal Crystal
Position 7
 
       
 

Now you can adjust each mechanism by grasping the small handle on top of each wheel and pulling it down for the required number of clicks. As the starting position on the mechanisms counts as 1, you need to adjust them as follows:
 

Pentagon: 3 clicks

Circle: 2 clicks

Hexagon: 6 clicks

 

If you make an error, each wheel has it's own little lever to reset it back to zero.
       
 








  Vibrating Crystal Puzzle
 

There is no denying it - this one's a bitch. It's a timed puzzle and the cursor hand interface doesn't help the situation.... but it can be done.

Here's the theory behind the puzzle. Now that you have 'tuned' Spire by adjusting the cables below, you need to get the four crystals vibrating at the correct frequencies as indicated by Sirrus in the flashbacks on the necklace and in his notes scattered all over Spire.

So you should have ascertained the following settings for each crystal:

 

Crystal: 6, 12, 4
Blue Crystal: 3, 1, 7
Rock: 10, 5, 5
Nara: 12, 12, 12

 

Okay, so you have 3 settings for each crystal, and 3 strings with sliders in front of you. Have a play with moving all 3 sliders up to the top of their strings (12 spaces each) and this will set the Nara crystal vibrating. Zoom in on the Nara and you can see that the crystal vibration time is determined by a wheel with holes turning around the crystal. Each crystal vibrates for a different amount of time.

 

So the aim is to enter the set the sliders to the settings for each of the four crystals and do it so that they are all vibrating at once. Some tricks are:

  • Enter the settings of the longest vibrating crystals first (as above, Crystal, Blue Crystal, Rock then Nara).


  • Be careful when moving the hand cursor. On the outer strings, it is very easy to move too close to the crystals and then the hand changes to a magnifying glass.



  • Instead of trying to count increments along the sliders, try counting the lights e.g. if a slider was in position 1 and you need to move it to position 5, just count 4 lights as you move it.


  • When moving a slider to a high or a low setting e.g. to 10, just slam that slider up to 12 and count back two lights - much quicker than counting out 5, 6, 7, 8, etc.


  • The crystals start vibrating when a 3rd number of their setting is entered but they dont require you to stop on that number - just sliding past it is enough. Therefore it is possible to slide past the third number to get that crystal vibrating and continue on to position the slider in place for the next crystal, essentially making two moves in one.
 

Make sense?? Here's how I did it........ and even with experience, it still took me 4 goes.
       
 








  The Crystal Platform
 

In this new area is a huge slab of stone blocking an elevator and a couple of gadgets. The panel with the symbol of a figurine doesn't provide alot of options - move the slider to the right.

Zoom in on the gadget to the left of the panel - a crystal figurine sits in the middle and spinning the wheel creates electricity. The necklace tells you that the crystals will hold a charge as long as they are contained.
However, the greenish bridge explosion in Tomahna and the crystals in the exploded linking chambers give an indication as to what might happen when the crystals are charged up and then grounded.
 

Have a look at the two containment units on the other side of the elevator. One is marked with 10-5-5 (stone) and the other with 12-12-12 (Nara). The necklace will show you the crystals falling down into the containment buckets. However, we want an explosion so the idea is to get a crystal to enter the containment unit but not land in a bucket. Feel free to try both but I can tell you its the nara that creates a great explosion so move the buckets between the containment units to the left.
 

Go back to the spider chair and press the blue button. We want to get a nara crystal so we need to create it's frequency. Set all three sliders to 12 and ka-boom!
 

Press the blue button to get out of the chair for the last time (hurrah!) and back across to the platform, where you will discover the elevator has now been cleared for use. Before you head off, note that there is a medallion hanging near the elevator lever - click on it to reveal 6 coloured dots and take a photo as you will use this information in the future. It's also important to note that the pattern is individual to each game, so here's a photo of the pattern from my latest game but it's useless to you. Get your own!!

 

So there you have it! Throw the lever and take a euphorically scored trip back to the top of Spire and head on home to Tomahna.
